Ethixplorer

  1. Ethics-Explorer: An ethic teaching tool that combines Ethical Dilemma Cards with Role Play Game

Game Objective

The objective of the game is to develop ethical decision-making skills by navigating through various engineering-related ethical dilemmas and experiencing the consequences of different choices.

Game Setup

a. Divide players into teams or individuals, depending on the number of participants.

b. Each team or player receives a set of Ethical Dilemma Cards and a device or platform to access the gamified simulations.

c. Set a time limit for each round or level, depending on the desired pace of the game.

Gameplay Flow

a. Start the game by explaining the rules, objectives, and flow to all participants.

b. Each round follows the following steps:

i. Card Draw: Players draw a card from the Ethical Dilemma Cards deck, which presents a scenario and an associated ethical dilemma.

ii. Discussion and Decision-Making: Players discuss the scenario within their teams or individually, considering various ethical perspectives, principles, and potential consequences.

iii. Decision Submission: After discussing the scenario, players submit their decision or choice based on their ethical analysis.

iv. Gamified Simulation: Players engage with the gamified simulation component, which reflects the consequences of their decisions. The simulation should showcase the impact of their choices on various stakeholders, the environment, or other relevant factors.

v. Reflection and Discussion: After experiencing the consequences in the simulation, players reflect on the outcomes, discuss the ethical implications of their decisions, and consider alternative courses of action.

vi. Scoring and Progression: Incorporating scoring or progression elements, record scores or allow players to advance to the next level based on the ethical reasoning demonstrated and the outcomes of decisions.

Ethical Dilemma Cards

a. Each ethical dilemma card corresponds to an engineering ethical issue.

b. Each engineering ethical issue involves multiple stakeholders.

Debriefing and Discussion

a. At the end of each round or level, facilitate a debriefing session where players discuss their experiences, insights, and lessons learned.

b. Encourage open dialogue, allowing players to share different perspectives and ethical justifications for their decisions.

c. Emphasize the complexity of ethical issues in engineering and the importance of critical thinking and holistic analysis.

Time Limit and Turns

a. Set a specific time limit for each round or level to ensure the game progresses smoothly. Adjust the time limit based on the complexity of the scenario and the depth of discussion required.

b. If playing in teams, implementing a turn-based system, allowing each team a specific amount of time for discussion and decision-making.

Scoring and Rewards

a. Assign points or scores based on the ethical reasoning, depth of discussion, or alignment with ethical principles.

b. Consider providing rewards, such as badges, achievements, or levels, to encourage engagement and progression.
